The Mystery of the Deep Ocean

Assessment details

Reading passage

Scientists continue to explore the deepest regions of the ocean, where unusual creatures survive in complete darkness. Some species communicate through bioluminescence, producing a pale blue light that can confuse predators or attract prey. During an expedition in the Pacific Ocean, researchers discovered an enormous transparent jellyfish and several microscopic organisms that had never been identified before. Although technology has improved dramatically, many underwater environments remain unexplored. Measurements suggest that parts of the ocean floor are deeper than Mount Everest is high. Because of these extraordinary conditions, every investigation can reveal surprising information about our planet.

A fluent KS3 reader should read this accurately, smoothly, and with appropriate expression in around 35–45 seconds.
